## Retention Technique Map — All 11 Techniques

### T1: Synergy (Thumbnail-Title-Hook) — 0:00-0:05

**Implementation:** Thumbnail shows a gleaming harbor packed with ships, twin pagodas in background, text overlay "SATIN." Title: something like "The Chinese Port That Named Satin." First 3 seconds of video: extreme wide of the same harbor, same visual composition as thumbnail. Instant confirmation — no bait-and-switch.

**Placement:** 0:00-0:05

### T2: Double Hook — 0:00-0:15

**Implementation:** Two-part hook.
- Part 1 (0:00-0:08): Visual stunner — extreme wide of Song Dynasty Quanzhou harbor, a hundred ships, every flag. Narration drops the "satin" bomb: "You're probably wearing something named after this city right now."
- Part 2 (0:08-0:15): Curiosity deepen — "This was the busiest port on Earth for three hundred years. Then it vanished."

**Placement:** 0:00-0:15

### T3: In Medias Res — 0:00-0:15

**Implementation:** Combined with Double Hook. We open at peak Quanzhou (Song/Yuan golden age), not at the beginning. The chronological story starts at 2:00. The opening drops the viewer into the most visually spectacular moment.

**Placement:** 0:00-0:15

### T4: Curiosity Gap + Hyper-Specificity — 0:15-2:00, recurring

**Implementation:**
- 0:15-2:00: "How did a fishing village on the Fujian coast become the largest port in the medieval world — and then disappear so completely that most people have never heard of it?" Hyper-specific: "34-meter ships with 13 watertight compartments. A mosque modeled after Damascus. Hindu temples from Tamil merchants. Seven religions in a single city."
- 6:30: "The man who ran this port would sell the entire Song dynasty to the Mongols. But that's later."
- 10:00: "Something is about to happen in this city that will destroy three centuries of coexistence in a single decade."

**Placement:** 0:15-2:00 (primary), 6:30, 10:00 (reinforcement)

### T5: Narrative Transportation — 2:00-16:30

**Implementation:** Three anchor characters, each tied to an era:
1. **The Fisherman** (Tang-Song, 2:00-5:00) — descendant of Minyue fishermen, watches bay transform from quiet cove to forest of masts
2. **The Porcelain Merchant** (Song Dynasty, 5:00-9:00) — born in Quanzhou, speaks Arabic and Tamil, loads Dehua white porcelain onto junks bound for the Indian Ocean
3. **The Arab Merchant's Wife** (Yuan Dynasty, 9:00-14:00) — mixed Arab-Chinese heritage, prays at Qingjing Mosque, sees everything destroyed in the Ispah Rebellion

Each character is introduced with sensory details (hands, clothing, daily routine). The audience lives each era through their eyes. The city changes around them.

**Placement:** Continuous through all eras, 2:00-14:00
