# MUSIC_CUES.md — Quanzhou: The Port That Named Satin

**Video:** Quanzhou — The Port That Named Satin (~17 min)
**Channel:** Cities Evolution
**Date:** 2026-04-09

**Overall Music Philosophy:** Music is a supporting layer, never competing with narration. Volume sits 10-15 dB below voice. Era-appropriate Chinese instrumentation establishes time and place. Silence is used deliberately at emotional turning points. The guzheng fisherman's theme threads through the entire score, transforming with each era.

---

## Scene 01 — Cold Hook (0:00-0:15)

**Music:** No music. Ambient-only opening. Maximum narration authority for the "satin" revelation.
**Instruments:** None
**Dynamics:** Pure silence at 0:00, then ambient fades in. At 0:10 ("three hundred years"), a single sustained low erhu note enters — almost subliminal. This note becomes the seed of the Era 1 theme.
**SFX:** Clip 01 (packed harbor): distant harbor cacophony — voices, wood creaking, water lapping, gulls. Clip 02 (empty harbor): abrupt silence — wind, one distant gull. The contrast IS the sound design.
**Transition:** Low erhu note sustains beneath the silence of the empty harbor.

---

## Scene 02 — Context & Curiosity Gap (0:15-2:00)

**Music:** Slow, contemplative guzheng drone building gradually. Sparse plucked notes — texture, not melody. 55-65 BPM. Soft dizi (transverse bamboo flute) enters at 1:00 ("the greatest port in the world"). At "It starts with a fisherman" (1:50), everything except solo guzheng drops away.
**Instruments:** Solo guzheng (sparse plucked notes), soft dizi (entering at 1:00), very soft frame drum (heartbeat pattern at 60 BPM, entering at 1:10)
**Dynamics:** Begins at near-silence (only the erhu seed from Scene 01). Guzheng enters at 0:20. Dizi at 1:00 ("the greatest port"). Frame drum pulse at 1:10 ("Seven faiths. A hundred ships."). Slight crescendo building to 1:35. At "It starts with a fisherman" (1:50), everything drops to solo guzheng — one note sustains, then 2 seconds near-silence.
**SFX:** Harbor water current (continuous, low). Distant ship timber creaking at 0:30. Wind.
**Transition:** Solo guzheng note sustains, then 2 seconds near-silence before Era 1.

---

## Scene 03 — The Fisherman's Bay (2:00-3:30)

**Music:** Solo guzheng melody — simple, pentatonic, folk-like. 60-65 BPM. The **"Fisherman's Theme"**: a four-note descending motif. Gentle, unhurried, intimate. Soft dizi joins at 2:45 (Kaiyuan Temple founded) with a warm sustained note — the first hint of something sacred entering the fisherman's world.
**Instruments:** Solo guzheng (melodic lead — the fisherman's theme), soft dizi (entering at 2:45), very faint xiao (vertical bamboo flute) drone on tonic
**Dynamics:** Guzheng enters alone at 2:05, playing the four-note motif. Repeats with slight variation. Volume stays low and constant — intimate, like the musician is sitting beside the narrator. At 2:45 ("Kaiyuan Temple is founded"), dizi enters — warmth, change arriving. At 3:10 ("Arab dhows anchor"), a brief oud-like pipa ornament — the first foreign sound. Returns to solo guzheng at 3:20.
**SFX:** Gentle waves on wooden hull (rhythmic, slow). Oarlock creaks — sparse, 2-3 isolated. Distant birds (2-3 calls). At 2:45, single temple bell — one tone, distant. At 3:10, creak of a larger vessel (dhow arriving — heavier timber sound).
**Transition:** Guzheng melody continues unbroken into Scene 04. SFX transitions from intimate harbor to growing settlement sounds.

---

## Scene 04 — The Port Awakens (3:30-5:00)

**Music:** Guzheng continues but gains a second voice — pipa (Chinese lute) joins in countermelody. Texture thickening as the city grows. 65-70 BPM — slightly faster than Scene 03. At 4:30 ("Shibosi"), soft Chinese frame drum enters — the pulse of bureaucratic order.
**Instruments:** Guzheng (continuing fisherman's theme), pipa (countermelody — more ornate, reflecting growing complexity), soft frame drum (entering at 4:30), very faint erhu (sustained harmony note)
**Dynamics:** Pipa enters at 3:40 ("harbor fills"). Music remains soft but noticeably fuller. At 4:00 ("Erythrina trees"), a brief flourish from the pipa — decorative, like the trees themselves. At 4:30 ("Shibosi"), frame drum enters with steady pulse — bureaucratic, ordered. At 4:50 ("license to become the busiest port"), slight crescendo, then hold.
**SFX:** Growing settlement: hammering, voices (more of them now), harbor bustle increasing. At 3:45, wind through Erythrina leaves (rustling). At 4:15, multiple ship sounds — larger vessels, heavier creaking. At 4:30, abacus clicking, paper rustling (Shibosi interior).
**Transition:** Music continues into Scene 05. Percussion sustains. The Song Dynasty golden age begins.

---

## Scene 05 — The Porcelain Merchant (5:00-6:30)

**Music:** Full orchestral texture — warm, processional. Chinese classical ensemble at its richest. The **"Merchant's Theme"**: an ornate pipa melody, more complex than the fisherman's guzheng motif — reflecting wealth and sophistication. 75-80 BPM.
**Instruments:** Pipa (melodic lead — the merchant's theme), guzheng (harmonic bed), erhu (warm sustained notes entering at 5:30), soft frame drum (steady pulse), occasional dizi ornaments
**Dynamics:** Music enters at medium volume at 5:00 — a clear step up in richness from Era 1. Pipa plays the merchant's theme — sinuous, ornamented. At 5:30 ("Dehua white porcelain"), erhu enters with a warm sustained note — the sound of craftsmanship. At 6:00 ("ocean-going vessels — thirty-four meters"), frame drum becomes more prominent — the pulse of industrial-scale trade. At 6:20 ("He thinks about the next shipment"), music softens to a reflective moment.
**SFX:** Market bustle (layered beneath music): merchants calling in Chinese and Arabic (indistinct), porcelain clinking (careful, delicate), footsteps on stone, fabric rustling (silk being handled). At 6:00, ship loading: ropes, wood, workers calling. At 6:15, river water (Jin River — the connection to the hinterland).
**Transition:** Music continues without break into Scene 06. Erhu sustains as religious diversity section begins.

---

## Scene 06 — Museum of World Religions (6:30-8:30)

**Music:** The most texturally complex passage in the video. A musical thesis of coexistence — each faith adds an instrument, all playing together by 7:30. Base: guzheng continues beneath. 70-80 BPM.
**Instruments (entering sequentially):**
- 6:30: Guzheng base (continuous)
- 6:35: Oud ornament (mosque — Arab/Persian sound)
- 6:50: Sitar-like pipa phrase (Hindu temple — Indian-inflected)
- 7:00: Large temple bell + singing bowl (Buddhist pagodas)
- 7:15: Solo xiao melody (Manichaean — ethereal, mysterious)
- 7:30: ALL instruments together — the coexistence ensemble
- 8:00: Erhu joins the ensemble (the Chinese heart beneath it all)

**Dynamics:** Each instrument enters soft and stays soft — no single tradition dominates. At 7:30, when all instruments play together, the total volume is medium — rich but controlled. This is the musical equivalent of "seven faiths in one city." At 8:00 ("He lives in the most religiously diverse city on Earth"), erhu takes a brief solo — grounding the diversity in Chinese identity. At 8:20, instruments begin thinning — preparing for Yuan Dynasty.
**SFX:** Muezzin call (distant, 6:35). Temple bell (7:00). Incense burning (subtle crackle). Street ambient with multiple languages. Wind through stone arches at 6:40 (Qingjing Mosque — the roofless quality even in its prime).
**Transition:** Instruments thin to guzheng and frame drum. Energy building toward Yuan Dynasty grandeur.

---

## Scene 07 — The World Arrives (8:30-9:30)

**Music:** Grandest texture yet — the city at its peak. Full Chinese classical ensemble plus Central Asian/Persian elements reflecting Pax Mongolica cosmopolitanism. 80-85 BPM.
**Instruments:** Full guzheng + pipa + erhu + dizi, oud (Persian/Arab presence), soft timpani-like drum, distant morin khuur (Mongol horsehead fiddle — subtle, establishing Yuan Dynasty context)
**Dynamics:** At 8:30, music swells to its richest point yet — matching the city's zenith. At 9:00 ("Ibn Battuta... could not count them all"), music sustains at forte — the sound of overwhelming abundance. At 9:10 ("a fourteenth-century facial recognition system"), brief pause in the music — pattern interrupt for the surprising detail. At 9:20 ("a man who will sell it all"), music darkens — oud shifts to minor, drums become ominous. Low erhu enters with a dissonant undertone.
**SFX:** Massive harbor ambient: hundreds of voices, wood, ropes, water, gulls — the busiest soundscape in the video. At 8:50, brush on silk (artist painting portrait). At 9:20, single low drum beat — foreboding.
**Transition:** L-cut: ominous drum pulse and erhu dissonance continue over Scene 08's opening.

---

## Scene 08 — The Arab Merchant's Wife (9:30-10:30)

**Music:** Intimate texture — solo oud melody with soft guzheng. The **"Coexistence Theme"**: warm, domestic, blending Arab and Chinese musical DNA. 65-70 BPM. The most tender music in the video.
**Instruments:** Solo oud (melodic lead — warm, personal), soft guzheng (harmonic bed), occasional frame drum (very soft, heartbeat-like)
**Dynamics:** At 9:30, the grand ensemble drops away — replaced by solo oud over guzheng. Deliberate textural contrast: the vastness of the port collapses to the intimacy of one home. Oud plays the coexistence theme — simple, beautiful, vulnerable. At 10:00 ("She speaks Hokkien at home and Arabic at prayer"), guzheng and oud play together in parallel — two traditions, one melody. At 10:15 ("everything she knows is about to end"), music darkens: oud shifts to minor, a low erhu enters with sustained dissonant note. Volume drops. Tension builds through quietness, not loudness.
**SFX:** Domestic sounds: tea being poured, ceramic on wood, children's voices (distant, happy), fabric rustling. Muezzin call at 9:45 (distant). At 10:15, sounds thin to near-silence — the world holding its breath.
**Transition:** J-cut at 10:20: low war drum begins under the domestic silence. The future arrives.

---

## Scene 09 — The Merchant Who Sold a Dynasty (10:30-12:00)

**Music:** Ascending tension — **Shepard Tone technique**. Layered rising string/erhu tones that never resolve. Continuous subconscious dread. 85-95 BPM, accelerating.
**Instruments:** Erhu (dark, winding melody — lead), layered rising strings (Shepard Tone effect), war drums (low, distant, entering at 10:50), pipa (sharp, percussive — punctuating treachery), guzheng (reduced to sparse, anxious notes)
**Dynamics:** At 10:30, erhu enters with dark melody — Pu Shougeng's theme. Layered rising strings begin the Shepard Tone effect — continuously ascending, never arriving. At 10:50 ("Mongol armies sweeping south"), war drums enter — distant but approaching. At 11:15 ("secret negotiations"), drums and strings build — maximum orchestral tension. At 11:30 ("confiscates Pu's entire fortune"), pipa strikes sharp, percussive notes — the sound of things breaking. At 11:45 ("the catastrophe is coming"), ALL music drops to a single sustained erhu note. Tension held, not released.
**SFX:** Abacus clicking (10:30 — Pu's counting house). At 10:50, distant combat (metal, horses, muffled). At 11:15, scroll unfurling (paper, wax seal). At 11:30, coins swept into chests (metallic cascade). At 11:45, wind — nothing else.
**Transition:** Single sustained erhu note holds. War drums re-enter beneath — the rumble of 1357 approaching.

---

## Scene 10 — The Ispah Rebellion (12:00-13:30)

**Music:** The video's sonic climax — and its most devastating silence.
**Instruments:** War drums (multiple, accelerating), erhu (keening solo after the silence), nothing else

**Dynamics (detailed — this is the emotional peak):**
- **12:00-12:15:** War drums from Scene 09 accelerate. Multiple frame drums, building intensity. 95-100 BPM — fastest tempo in the video.
- **12:15-12:25:** ("fighting spreads... the region tears itself apart") Drums reach maximum intensity. A clashing metallic percussion accent on each phrase.
- **12:25:** ("the foreign quarters fall silent") **SUDDEN SILENCE. Complete cut.** 3 seconds of absolute nothing. The most important sonic moment in the entire video. This silence IS the massacre — what we cannot show, the sound design communicates through absence.
- **12:30-13:00:** Solo erhu enters — the saddest melody in the video. Keening, isolated, slow. 50 BPM. No accompaniment. The erhu plays a variation of the coexistence theme from Scene 08, but broken — notes spaced further apart, intervals more painful.
- **13:00-13:15:** ("Qingjing Mosque loses its roof") Erhu continues. A single stone echo (SFX) punctuates the silence.
- **13:15-13:30:** ("her world is gone") Erhu plays its final phrase — a descending line that never resolves. Thins to a single held note.

**SFX:** At 12:00, distant conflict (muffled — not graphic). At 12:25, TOTAL SILENCE — 3 seconds. At 12:30, wind begins (gentle, persistent). At 13:00, a single stone falling in the roofless mosque (echo). At 13:15, wind through empty stone arches.
**Transition:** Erhu thins to nothing. 3 seconds of near-silence before Scene 11.

## Scene 11 — The Sea Ban (13:30-14:30)

**Music:** Solo xiao (vertical bamboo flute) — the loneliest instrument in the Chinese ensemble. A simple descending melody that echoes the fisherman's guzheng theme from Scene 03, but transformed: slower, sadder, in a lower register. 50-55 BPM.
**Instruments:** Solo xiao (primary — breathy, intimate, solitary), brief guzheng + pipa + dizi at 14:00 (Zheng He's fleet — a memory of grandeur)
**Dynamics:** Xiao enters at 13:30 — completely alone. The emptiest texture since Scene 01. At 13:50 ("the harbor silts"), the xiao's melody descends further — the sound of things going quiet. At 14:00 ("Zheng He brings his treasure fleet"), a brief 15-second swell: guzheng, pipa, and dizi return — the Song Dynasty ensemble playing one last time, like a ghost. Full, warm, but brief. At 14:15, they fade. Xiao alone again. At 14:25 ("goes quiet with it"), xiao plays its final note — sustained, then fading.
**SFX:** Wind (continuous — the dominant sound now). Water — quieter than before. At 13:45, sand shifting (siltation — a subtle granular sound). At 14:00, massive ship timber and rigging (Zheng He's fleet — a last burst of harbor sound, rich and brief). At 14:20, silence returns.
**Transition:** Xiao sustains, fades. Near-silence.

## Scene 12 — The Diaspora (14:30-15:30)

**Music:** Guzheng returns — the fisherman's theme from Scene 03. But it has aged: slower, more ornate, with more vibrato. Soft erhu harmony beneath. 60-65 BPM. At 15:00, pipa and dizi join briefly — the full Song Dynasty ensemble playing as a memory, quieter than before.
**Instruments:** Guzheng (fisherman's theme — aged reprise), soft erhu (harmony), brief pipa + dizi at 15:00 (memory swell)
**Dynamics:** At 14:30, guzheng enters alone with the fisherman's theme — recognition for the audience. The melody is the same four notes but richer, sadder, wiser. Erhu joins at 14:40 — warmth beneath. At 15:00 ("they carried the city with them"), pipa and dizi join for a 15-second swell — the coexistence ensemble one more time, faint, like hearing music from a distant room. At 15:15, pipa and dizi fade. Guzheng and erhu continue.
**SFX:** Ship departure (ropes cast off, wood, water). At 14:50, modern Chinatown ambient (distant Hokkien speech, temple bells, traffic). At 15:10, modern Quanzhou (birdsong, distant traffic, incense crackling).
**Transition:** Music thins to solo guzheng. The fisherman's theme — heading toward its final statement.

## Scene 13 — The Port That Named Satin (15:30-17:00)

**Music:** The climax of the musical architecture. The fisherman's guzheng theme, fully orchestrated for the first and only time. All character themes converge. Begins intimate, builds to the fullest sound in the video (at "satin"), then resolves to peace.
**Instruments:** Full synthesis — guzheng (fisherman's theme), pipa (merchant's theme), oud (coexistence theme), erhu (the Chinese heart), dizi, xiao. All character themes present, layered, interacting.

**Dynamics (detailed — the musical thesis):**
- **15:30-15:45:** Solo guzheng plays the fisherman's theme — one final time, alone. Slower than ever. Recognition.
- **15:45-16:00:** ("The Qingjing Mosque still stands") Erhu joins. Then pipa. Instruments layering in, one by one — the city's musical DNA reassembling.
- **16:00-16:10:** ("Zayton. The Arabic name.") Oud enters — the Arab world's voice returning. The coexistence theme weaving into the fisherman's theme. All three character themes now playing simultaneously for the first and only time.
- **16:10-16:20:** ("satin... in French and English") Full ensemble at forte — the richest, fullest moment of the entire score. The guzheng plays the fisherman's theme, pipa plays the merchant's theme, oud plays the coexistence theme. The musical thesis: three eras, three characters, one city, one word.
- **16:20-16:35:** ("Every time someone says the word satin") Slow decrescendo. Instruments drop out one by one — first drums, then dizi, then pipa, then oud. Erhu and guzheng remain.
- **16:35-16:45:** ("Quanzhou is gone as a great port. But its name is in your closet.") Solo guzheng — the fisherman's theme one last time. Plays its final phrase. Sustains on the tonic. Resolution.
- **16:45-16:50:** ("Malacca") A single new note from a different instrument (gamelan-like metallic tone — Southeast Asian teaser). Brief, bright, forward-pulling. Then gone.
- **16:50-17:00:** Guzheng note thinning to nothing. Silence. Fade.

**SFX:** Modern temple ambient (bells, distant traffic, birdsong) at 15:30. At 16:00, fabric sound — satin sliding against itself (subtle, specific, the physical reality of the word). At 16:35, water — Quanzhou Bay, one last time. Final 10 seconds: only water. Then silence.
**Transition:** Fade to complete silence over final 5 seconds. No music, no SFX. Black screen. End card.

## Leitmotif Reference

Four character leitmotifs thread through the score and converge in the climax:

1. **Fisherman's Theme:** Four-note descending motif, first heard on solo guzheng (Scene 03, 2:00). The backbone of the score. Reprised on guzheng in Scenes 11 (echo via xiao), 12 (aged reprise), and 13 (full orchestration). Pentatonic, folk-like. Associated with dawn, solitude, the bay, continuity.

2. **Merchant's Theme:** Ornamented pipa melody, first heard in Scene 05 (5:00). More complex than the fisherman's theme — reflecting sophistication. Reprised in Scenes 12 (memory) and 13 (climax). Associated with trade, abundance, Tumen Street.

3. **Coexistence Theme:** Warm oud melody over guzheng, first heard in Scene 08 (9:30). Blends Arab and Chinese musical DNA. Destroyed in Scene 10 (erhu plays broken version after the massacre). Restored in Scene 13 (16:00) when oud returns. Associated with the Arab merchant's wife, diversity, home.

4. **Solitude Theme:** Solo xiao, first heard in Scene 11 (13:30). Echoes the fisherman's theme but descending, slower, lonelier. Not reprised — it exists only in the decline. Associated with loss, emptiness, the sea ban.

All themes except Solitude play simultaneously at 16:10-16:20 — the musical thesis statement.
