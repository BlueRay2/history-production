# HOOK Visual Prompts — Paris Eiffel Cold Hook v2 (Numbers Shock)

**Hook narration (15s, EN):**
"Eighteen thousand thirty-eight iron pieces. Two and a half million rivets. Three hundred workers. Zero deaths during construction. [pause] The Eiffel Tower is one hundred thirty-seven years old — and still the most visited paid monument in the world. [pause] But it was meant to stand for only twenty."

4 shots × ~3.5s, 9:16 vertical (1080×1920), 60fps, Patrick International cinematic grade.
All shots support adding a **reference image of the Eiffel Tower** — see "Reference image usage" at end.

---

## Shot 1 (0:00–0:04) — "18,038 iron pieces"

**Recommended service:** Sora 2 Pro (cinematic + image cameo).
**Reference image:** front-facing photo of Eiffel Tower at full frame.

> Cinematic 9:16 vertical, ultra slow dolly-in, starting on a single rust-colored iron beam in pitch black, then revealing — through pulsing light cones — that the camera is INSIDE the lattice of the Eiffel Tower at night. Camera flies upward between iron crosses at 0.5x speed. Each iron piece is razor-sharp, backlit by cold white-blue moonlight. Number "18,038" materializes letter-by-letter in mid-air ahead of camera, glowing ember-orange, hangs for 0.7s, then disintegrates into iron dust as camera passes through it. No people. Zero atmospheric haze. 4K, 60fps, Patrick International cinematic grade. Use reference image as lighting/silhouette anchor.

---

## Shot 2 (0:04–0:08) — "2.5M rivets / 300 workers / 0 deaths"

**Recommended service:** Sora 2 Pro (human subjects + period detail).
**Reference image:** historical 1888 photo of Eiffel construction, or any reference of the iron lattice geometry.

> Cinematic 9:16 vertical, locked-off medium shot, 1888 sepia archival aesthetic graded toward warm amber and deep shadow. A SINGLE Parisian construction worker, mid-thirties, in oil-stained linen shirt and flat cap, hangs from a leather harness 200 meters above Champ de Mars, hammering a glowing-red rivet into iron lattice. Sparks fly diagonally across frame. His grip is dirty, his expression focused, no fear. Slow push-in over 4 seconds. Numbers stack vertically right side of frame in elegant white serif: "2,500,000" → "300" → "0", each appearing on a new hammer impact. The "0" lingers. 4K, 60fps. Patrick International cinematic grade. Use reference image to pin the lattice geometry; replace people with the described worker.

---

## Shot 3 (0:08–0:12) — "137 years / most visited paid monument"

**Recommended service:** Flow Veo 3.1 (atmospheric establishing — free on Ultra plan).
**Reference image:** Eiffel Tower at night with golden light, full frame.

> Cinematic 9:16 vertical, hyperlapse from blue hour to full night, 4K, ultra-wide. Eiffel Tower center frame, fully golden-lit. Streams of glowing tourist crowd ripples around the base like a river of phone-screens, time-compressed so individual people blur into light tracks. The hourly searchlight beacon at the top sweeps once across frame, perfectly timed to land on the cut. Subtle Paris haze, distant Sacré-Cœur visible upper-left. Number "137" rises from the base of the tower like smoke, holds for 0.6s at platform-2 level, dissipates upward. 60fps. Patrick International cinematic grade. Use reference image as base — keep tower silhouette identical, animate environment.

---

## Shot 4 (0:12–0:15) — "Meant for only 20"

**Recommended service:** Kling 3.0 (stylized macro, image-to-video stellar).
**Reference image:** antique 1885 architectural blueprint of Eiffel Tower (or photo from French national archives).

> Cinematic 9:16 vertical, extreme macro on antique 1885 architectural blueprint of Eiffel Tower, parchment yellowed at edges, blueprint lines in faded indigo. Camera slowly pushes in (90mm equivalent, very shallow DOF) on a single corner of the page where a red wax-stamp inscription reads "TEMPORAIRE — 20 ANS" in faded cinnabar red. Dust motes float across a beam of warm oil-lamp light coming from frame-right. As camera pushes deepest, the red stamp BLEEDS — the ink seems to expand and crack the paper around it. End on the stamp dominating 80% of frame. 4K, 60fps. Patrick International cinematic grade. Use reference image (blueprint) as the entire base layer; only animate the bleed and the dust.
