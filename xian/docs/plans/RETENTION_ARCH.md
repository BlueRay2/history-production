## Structural decision: 3-act arc (Phase 0 open question #1+#2 resolved)

**Chosen:** 3 acts, not 4. The Ming city-wall rebuild (1370s) and modern industrial era don't carry enough narrative stakes to sustain their own act; they live as grace notes. The Xi'an Incident (1936) IS included — it's a "pivotal moment that changed WWII" and pays off the "one city holds world-scale stories" theme.

| Act | Time | Era | Emotional beat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I — The Buried Emperor** | 0:00–3:00 | Qin Shi Huang, Terracotta Army, 1974 discovery | Awe + mystery (open loop on sealed tomb) |
| **II — The Open City** | 3:00–6:00 | Han + Tang Chang'an, Silk Road, religions, Nestorian stele | Surprise — Xi'an was the centre of the world |
| **III — The Kidnapping** | 6:00–8:30 | Ming walls (brief), Xi'an Incident 1936, modern Xi'an | Stakes close — one city, three hinges of history |

Total 7-10 min range, target ~8:30. Buffer 1-1:30 for music swells, pauses, visual beats without narration.

## Cold open (0:00–0:15) — Terracotta Army anchor

**Script draft:**
> *"In 1974, a farmer digging a well in northwest China struck something hard. He'd just found the private army of China's first emperor — eight thousand life-size clay soldiers, each with a different face, entombed for two thousand two hundred years. Two kilometres away lies his mausoleum, still sealed, because the soil there runs with mercury. This is the story of Xi'an — the city where the First Emperor still keeps his court. And that's only the first of three global turning points that happened here."*

**Retention techniques layered:**
- **Double Hook** (0:00–0:05): visual shock (farmer's shovel → warrior's face revealed) + value promise ("first of three global turning points")
- **Synergy** (0:00–0:03): first words pay off whatever thumbnail says (expected thumbnail: Terracotta Army face + text "1974")
- **Curiosity Gap + Hyper-Specificity** ("eight thousand", "two thousand two hundred years", "two kilometres"): concrete numbers prevent abstraction
- **Zeigarnik Open Loop OL-1**: the mercury-sealed tomb is named but not resolved — payoff at end of Act I

## Open questions resolved (from Phase 0 §8)

| Q | Resolution |
|---|---|
| 1. Narrative focus | Linear 3-act with Terracotta Army as cold-open AND Act I, not as scattered framing device. |
| 2. Era balance | Qin 35% / Han+Tang 35% / 1936+modern 30%. |
| 3. Cold open length | 15 seconds hook + 45 seconds setup/CTA-1 through 1:00 title card. |
| 4. Visual style | CGI for architectural reconstructions (Chang'an grid, Daming Palace); stock + modern Xi'an drone for transitions; period-recreations hand-crafted for 3 key beats (Yang Zhifa, Sima Qian, Chiang kidnapping). |
| 5. Expert interviews | No — pure narrative with inline source citation (Portal 2007, Sima Qian, Whitfield). |
| 6. Takeaway | "One city has been the accidental centre of three global histories." Gives viewer a memorable compression. |
| 7. Russian track | Deferred to Phase 6 SEO, contingent on performance of English master. |
| 8. Anti-clickbait tone | Tackle pop-culture myths **once, directly**: single sentence in Act I's chrome-debunk beat mentions *The Mummy* and moves on. |
| 9. Music | Traditional Chinese instrumentation (erhu, guqin, pipa) blended with cinematic orchestral bed. Avoid synth or electronic. |
| 10. Primary "surprise" | **The scale of Tang Chang'an** (1 million people, cosmopolitan, 5 religions). Chrome-debunk is a pattern-interrupt, Nestorian stele is a reveal, but the Tang scale is the structural payoff of Act II. |
