# Audience Persona — Xi'an documentary

**Phase:** 1 · **Author:** Claude · **Date:** 2026-04-22 · **Input:** `xian/docs/KNOWLEDGE_SUMMARY.md` (Gemini Phase 0).

## Primary — "The Curious Lay Historian"

Anglo adult, 28-55, college-educated, non-specialist in Chinese history. Watches documentary YouTube in 15-min evening slots. Has a **pop-culture baseline** for Xi'an via *The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor* (2008), *Tomb Raider II* (1997), and passing travel-feed exposure. Does NOT read academic history. Reacts to **specific concrete facts** (dates, counts, names) over abstract claims.

**Triggers (what hooks this persona):**
- **"Hidden continuity"** — long timelines revealed through a single site (the well → Terracotta Army → still-sealed emperor's tomb two km away).
- **"Science corrects myth"** — debunks that viewer didn't know to doubt (chrome-plating of bronze weapons is NOT an ancient preservation technique; 2019 Scientific Reports paper disproved the 40-year myth).
- **"Human-scale moment inside an epic"** — Yang Zhifa's shovel, Sima Qian's candlelit writing, the anonymous artisan painting a warrior's eyes.
- **"Global connection they didn't expect"** — Nestorian Christians in 635 CE China, a Syriac stele, a mosque built in 742 CE, Sogdian taverns where Li Bai drank. Xi'an as terminus of the Silk Road.

**Tone preferences:**
- **Specific over grand** — "700,000 workers" beats "a vast labour force".
- **Admit what's unknown** — the central tomb is unexcavated because of mercury contamination, not "because of ancient curses". Viewers respect intellectual honesty.
- **Avoid** tired clickbait registers: "MYSTERY", "SECRET", "you won't believe", "INSANE".

**What they know / think they know** (per Phase 0 §5):
- Terracotta Army = lots of statues in China, buried with an emperor
- Pop-culture mental model: cursed/magical soldiers that can wake up (The Mummy 3, Tomb Raider)
- Xi'an = tourist destination

**What they don't know but would find revelatory:**
- The warriors were **brightly painted** (colors preserved briefly after excavation, then oxidised within minutes)
- The discovery was an **accident** by a farmer digging a well in 1974
- The **main tomb is STILL sealed** and soil tests confirm mercury levels 100× background
- Xi'an-as-Chang'an was the **world's largest city** under the Tang (~1 million, when contemporary London was ~10k)
- Early **Christianity and Islam** both landed in Xi'an centuries before Western convention expects

## Secondary — "The China-Curious Traveller"

Anglo or Russian-speaking adult who has Xi'an in "places to visit someday" mental list — maybe booked a China tour, watched Mulan (2020), followed travel YouTube. Weaker commitment to historical depth, stronger pull to aesthetic + food + walkability.

**Triggers:**
- Visuals: Muslim Quarter street food, Bell Tower at night, cycling on Ming walls, cave-temple Buddhism (Bingling, Maijishan nearby).
- "Still alive today" — the Hui Muslim community has lived there 1300 years, not a museum exhibit.

**Tone preferences:** atmospheric, sensory, first-person walk-throughs rather than lectures.

**Script implication:** secondary persona gets 10-15% of screen time (the Tang cosmopolitan beat + Muslim Quarter architectural detail). Primary persona drives the 70%+ historical spine.

## Tertiary — "History-of-Ideas Reader"

A Need-for-Cognition sub-slice of primary — gets retention-pinned by conceptual reveals (e.g., "the idea that one city could simultaneously host Nestorian, Zoroastrian, Muslim, Buddhist, and Taoist worship in 750 CE" reframes how they think about religious pluralism). Doesn't need more visuals, needs **correct claims** and **named sources**.

**Script implication:** one dedicated ~60-90s beat exploring Tang cosmopolitanism with the Nestorian stele as the concrete object. Source-name a historian inline (Victor Xiong or Susan Whitfield) to signal academic register without breaking flow.

## Anti-persona (who we EXCLUDE and what we don't do)

- **"Conspiracy history" TikTok audience** — ancient aliens built the army, mercury rivers prove lost technology, etc. We explicitly DEBUNK rather than coyly-gesture, to signal our register.
- **"Crash course YouTube"** — 10-min-a-dynasty rapid overviews. Our advantage is the SINGLE-CITY depth.

## Language / subtitle policy

- **Primary audio:** English.
- **Sub-titles:** English burn-in for Chinese names on first appearance (Qin Shi Huang 秦始皇 / Yang Zhifa / Chang'an 长安). Russian sub-track generated if performance warrants (defer Phase 6 SEO).
