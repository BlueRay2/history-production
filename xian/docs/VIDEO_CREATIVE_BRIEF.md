# Video Creative Brief — Xi'an

**Phase:** 1 (consolidation) · **Author:** Claude · **Date:** 2026-04-22.

## One-line premise

Xi'an (西安) is the single city on Earth that has been the accidental centre of three world-scale histories: the First Emperor's eternal army (210 BCE, discovered 1974), the most cosmopolitan capital of the medieval world (Tang Chang'an, ~750 CE, 1 million people, 5 religions), and the kidnapping that forced China into its alliance against imperial Japan (December 1936) — altering the course of WWII.

## Working title (for Phase 6 SEO)

**Xi'an: The City That Was the Centre of History Three Times**
(alternates: *"Three Buried Capitals"* / *"The Farmer Who Found an Empire"*)

## Cold open (locked)

> *"In 1974, a farmer digging a well in northwest China struck something hard. He'd just found the private army of China's first emperor — eight thousand life-size clay soldiers, each with a different face, entombed for two thousand two hundred years. Two kilometres away lies his mausoleum, still sealed, because the soil there runs with mercury. This is the story of Xi'an — the city where the First Emperor still keeps his court. And that's only the first of three global turning points that happened here."*

## Anti-clickbait register

- No "MYSTERY", "SECRET", "INSANE", "YOU WON'T BELIEVE".
- Named sources inline (Sima Qian Shiji, Portal 2007, Scientific Reports 2019 chrome paper, Whitfield Silk Road histories).
- Pop-culture pokes happen ONCE (chrome-myth beat ~2:45 acknowledges *The Mummy: Tomb of the Dragon Emperor* by name, then corrects the record in 15 seconds).

## Primary reveal (structural payoff)

**Tang Chang'an was the world's largest city, ~1 million people, 5 religions in working worship, when contemporary London was ~10,000.** This reframes how the viewer thinks about medieval world centers. Positioned as Act II climax.

## Key facts that MUST appear (phase 2 SCRIPT compliance checklist)

- 1974 / Yang Zhifa / Lintong / farmer digging a well
- 700,000 workers (Sima Qian Shiji)
- ~8,000 figures (current estimate post-excavation)
- Originally painted, colors oxidised minutes after air exposure
- Chrome-plating is a MYTH — 2019 Scientific Reports paper; preservation is bronze alloy + soil chemistry
- Mercury soil readings 100× background confirm Shiji's mercury-rivers account
- Mausoleum unexcavated — modern preservation choice, not ancient curse
- Tang Chang'an population ~1M, grid plan
- Western Market — Sogdians, Persians, Arabs, Turks, Indians, Japanese, Koreans
- Nestorian stele 781 CE, Syriac inscription, Alopen arrived 635 CE
- Great Mosque founded 742 CE, Chinese-temple form with Mecca alignment
- Daming Palace 4.5× Forbidden City
- 1936 Xi'an Incident — Chiang Kai-shek kidnapped at Huaqing Pool, Second United Front result
- Wu Zetian ruled from Xi'an (only female emperor of China) — optional grace note

## Key facts that MUST NOT appear

- Any claim of "ancient chrome-plating technology" EXCEPT to debunk it (scientific consensus 2019).
- Any claim that the central tomb contains "treasures" or "mysteries" beyond what Sima Qian documented — we lead with the documented account + modern mercury evidence, avoid speculation.
- Any "ancient aliens" / "lost technology" / "curse" framing, even ironically.
