# Competitor Analysis Plan — Istanbul: The Port Between Two Worlds

**Video:** Istanbul — The Port Between Two Worlds (15-17 min)
**Channel:** Cities Evolution
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Phase:** Pre-production competitor research

## 1. Video Selection Criteria

### Selection Priority (in order)

**Priority 1 — Istanbul/Constantinople videos from the 5 direct competitors**

Search each competitor channel for videos specifically about Istanbul, Constantinople, or Byzantium:

| # | Channel | Handle | Search Terms |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Majestic AI Studio | @MajesticAIStudio | Istanbul, Constantinople, Byzantium, Ottoman, Byzantine |
| 2 | Restored Timeline | @RestoredTimeline | Istanbul, Constantinople, Byzantium, Ottoman, Byzantine |
| 3 | Tim Reborn History | @Tim_Reborn_History | Istanbul, Constantinople, Byzantium, Ottoman, Byzantine |
| 4 | Resurrected History | @resurrectedhistory10mv | Istanbul, Constantinople, Byzantium, Ottoman, Byzantine |
| 5 | Reconstruction History | @ReconstructionHistory | Istanbul, Constantinople, Byzantium, Ottoman, Byzantine |

**Priority 2 — City evolution videos from the same 5 competitors**

If a competitor has no Istanbul-specific content, select their best-performing city evolution video (by view count). Preference for:
- Port cities / trade hub cities (closest format parallel)
- Ancient/medieval cities (same temporal range)
- Videos in the 10-20 minute range (same format length)

**Priority 3 — Top-performing AI history reconstruction videos about ancient/medieval cities (any channel)**

Search YouTube for high-view-count videos matching these criteria:
- AI-generated or AI-reconstructed historical content
- About ancient or medieval cities (Rome, Athens, Alexandria, Baghdad, Venice, etc.)
- Published within the last 12 months (recent = relevant algorithm signals)
- 10-20 minutes in length
- 100K+ views preferred

**Priority 4 — Stylistic reference videos (non-AI, high retention)**

If the above yield fewer than 5 strong candidates, supplement with high-performing history documentaries that match our format aspirations:
- Who Paid for Art (@WhoPaidforArt) — history-through-economics angle
- Scott's History (@ScottsHistory) — personal delivery style
- Any history channel with exceptionally strong retention on 15+ minute videos

### Target Count

**5-10 videos total**, distributed as:
- 2-4 from direct competitors (Priority 1-2)
- 2-3 from broader AI history reconstruction space (Priority 3)
- 1-2 from stylistic references (Priority 4)
- Minimum 5 videos required before proceeding to synthesis

### Selection Disqualifiers

Do NOT include videos that are:
- Under 5 minutes (different format, different retention dynamics)
- Over 30 minutes (different audience commitment profile)
- Non-English (our content is English-language)
- Shorts/vertical format
- Compilations without original narration
- Older than 24 months (outdated algorithm signals)

## 3. Cross-Video Synthesis

After all individual analysis cards are completed, produce a synthesis document.

**Output file:** `istanbul/docs/research/competitors/COMPETITOR_SYNTHESIS.md`

### Synthesis Structure

#### 3.1 Hook Pattern Analysis

- **Most common hook type** across analyzed videos
- **Highest-performing hook type** (correlated with view count and estimated retention)
- **Hook patterns specific to historical/city content** (vs. general YouTube hooks)
- **Recommended hook approach for Istanbul** based on findings

#### 3.2 Structure Winning Formulas

- **Dominant structure pattern** (chronological vs. thematic vs. character-driven)
- **Optimal section count and length** for 15-17 minute historical content
- **Where do most videos place their strongest content?** (front-loaded vs. distributed vs. climax-heavy)
- **Era transition best practices** — what works, what doesn't

#### 3.3 Retention Device Effectiveness Ranking

Rank all identified retention techniques by:
1. Frequency of use across videos
2. Correlation with estimated retention quality
3. Execution quality range (how well do competitors use each technique?)
4. Applicability to Cities Evolution format

Produce a prioritized list: "Must use", "Should use", "Optional", "Avoid (overused/ineffective)"

#### 3.4 Visual Quality Benchmarks

- **Current quality floor** — what is the minimum AI visual quality that viewers accept in this niche?
- **Quality ceiling** — the best AI visuals seen across competitors
- **Where Cities Evolution should aim** — and which AI service best delivers that level
- **Style coherence** — do any competitors maintain consistent visual style across clips? How?

#### 3.5 Content Gaps and Opportunities

- **What are competitors NOT doing?** (human-centered storytelling? Daily life focus? Bosphorus as motif?)
- **Underserved sub-topics** — aspects of Istanbul/Constantinople that no competitor has covered well
- **Format innovations** — any structural approaches competitors haven't tried that Cities Evolution could pioneer?

#### 3.6 Aggregate Recommendations for Istanbul Script

A prioritized list of 5-10 actionable recommendations for the scriptwriter, derived from the synthesis. Each recommendation includes:
- The finding it's based on
- Which competitor videos provided the evidence
- How to implement it in the Istanbul script specifically

## 4. Tools and Workflow

### Primary Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Invocation |
|---|---|---|
| **youtube-transcript** | Download transcript text for structural analysis, hook breakdown, retention device identification | `/youtube-transcript <url>` |
| **video-analyzer** | Extract frames at key timestamps for visual quality assessment, transition analysis, AI artifact identification | `/video-analyzer <video_path>` (requires downloaded video) |
| **WebSearch** | Find competitor videos, check view counts, discover relevant channels beyond the known 5 | WebSearch tool |
| **article-extractor** | Extract any written analysis or blog posts about competitor channels | `/article-extractor <url>` |

### Workflow Steps

```
Phase 1: Video Discovery and Selection
├── Search each of the 5 competitor channels for Istanbul/Constantinople content
├── Search each competitor for their best city evolution video
├── WebSearch for top AI history reconstruction videos (broader pool)
├── Apply selection criteria and disqualifiers
├── Compile final list of 5-10 videos
└── Output: VIDEO_SELECTION_LIST.md

Phase 2: Individual Analysis
├── For each selected video:
│   ├── youtube-transcript to download transcript
│   ├── Read transcript: analyze hook, section structure, retention devices
│   ├── Note transition patterns from transcript + timestamps
│   ├── Estimate retention curve from structural analysis
│   ├── (Optional) video-analyzer for visual quality spot-checks
│   └── Write analysis card: CA-{n}_{channel}_{title}.md
└── Output: 5-10 analysis cards in istanbul/docs/research/competitors/

Phase 3: Cross-Video Synthesis
├── Read all analysis cards
├── Aggregate patterns (hooks, structure, retention, transitions)
├── Identify winning formulas and gaps
├── Produce ranked recommendations
└── Output: COMPETITOR_SYNTHESIS.md

Phase 4: Integration with Script Planning
├── Feed synthesis into scriptwriter briefing
├── Map specific recommendations to Istanbul video structure
└── Output: Recommendations section in COMPETITOR_SYNTHESIS.md
```

### Time Estimates

| Total individual analysis (5-10 videos) | 2-6 hours | Number of videos selected |

## 5. Fallback Strategies

### If transcript is unavailable

Some videos may not have subtitles (manual or auto-generated). Fallback procedure:

| Step | Action | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Try `--write-sub` (manual subtitles) | Best |
| 2 | Try `--write-auto-sub` (YouTube auto-generated) | Good |
| 3 | Analyze via **metadata only**: title, description, timestamps in description, pinned comments, chapter markers | Partial — enough for structure and hook analysis |
| 4 | **WebSearch** for third-party analysis or reactions to the video | Supplementary |
| 5 | **Watch and manually note** key timestamps, section transitions, hook type (if video is accessible) | Time-consuming but thorough |

### If competitor channels have no relevant content

If a direct competitor has zero videos about cities, urban history, or related topics:
- Note the gap in the selection list
- Replace with a Priority 3 video (broader AI history space)
- Document: "Channel X excluded — no relevant content found as of [date]"

### If fewer than 5 videos can be found

- Lower the view count threshold (50K+ instead of 100K+)
- Expand the time window (up to 36 months instead of 12)
- Include stylistic reference videos from non-AI history channels
- Minimum viable analysis: 3 videos with full analysis cards + synthesis
