# Edit Plan — Istanbul: The Port Between Two Worlds

**Video:** Istanbul — The Port Between Two Worlds (15-17 min)
**Channel:** Cities Evolution

## 1. Editing Passes

The script undergoes four sequential editing passes, each with distinct focus and acceptance criteria. Passes are applied per batch (see Section 3), not to the full script at once.

### Pass 1 — Developmental Edit

**Focus:** Structure, narrative arc, pacing, emotional trajectory, completeness.

**The editor examines:**
- Does the scene breakdown match the Script Structure Plan (16 scenes, 4 acts)?
- Is the narrative arc intact — does emotional intensity follow the planned trajectory (warmth → grandeur → tension → transcendence)?
- Are all three characters introduced with sufficient depth and sensory detail?
- Are era transitions clean — does each J-cut bridge work?
- Are all 6 open loops planted and resolved at the correct scenes?
- Is the Bosphorus "eternal thread" motif woven through all eras, not just stated in the climax?
- Does the climax (Scene 15) deliver the key insight with genuine weight, not just repeat the thesis from Scene 02?
- Is the pacing varied — does the breathe-build-release rhythm work when read aloud?
- Are any scenes too thin (under-developed) or too heavy (information overload)?

**Acceptance criteria for Pass 1:**

| Criterion | Standard | Pass/Fail Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Scene count | 14-18 scenes | Scenes can be merged or split if narrative demands |
| Narrative arc | All 3 characters introduced within first 40% of runtime | Character intros at Scenes 03, 07, 12 |
| Open loops | All loops planted and resolved per MARKETING_PLAN map | Each loop traceable in text |
| Pacing | No scene exceeds 2:00 except Scene 15 (climax, up to 2:00) | Read-aloud timing check |
| Emotional trajectory | Intensity rises across eras, peaks at Scene 15 | Subjective editorial judgment |
| Thesis delivery | Key insight stated at climax, not front-loaded | Scene 15 contains the definitive formulation |
| Era balance | Each era receives 3-4 minutes; no era dominates | Within 30s of duration targets |
| Completeness | All mandatory content points from NICHE_STYLE_GUIDE Section 9 present | Checklist verification |

**Output:** Structural feedback document with scene-level notes. Rewrite requests for scenes that fail criteria.

---

### Pass 2 — Line Edit

**Focus:** Sentence-level flow, tone consistency, emotional resonance, voice quality.

**The editor examines:**
- Does every sentence sound like it belongs in a BBC documentary narrated by someone who personally knew these people?
- Is formality at 5/10 — literary but warm, not academic?
- Is emotionality at 8/10 — does the reader/listener FEEL the fisherman's dawn, the merchant's anxiety, the craftsman's determination?
- Are transitions between panoramic overview and intimate character perspective smooth?
- Is sentence variety adequate — no more than 2 sentences of the same length/structure in sequence?
- Are strong, active verbs used? (Editor skill: "has the ability to" → "can"; "make a decision" → "decide")
- Is there sensory immersion in every character scene — sight, sound, touch, smell?
- Does the "viewer positioning" technique work naturally ("Stand beside him...")?
- Are ElevenLabs audio tags placed correctly and sparingly — tags only where natural reading would miss the intended delivery?

**Acceptance criteria for Pass 2:**

| Criterion | Standard | Pass/Fail Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Tone consistency | Every paragraph sounds like the same narrator | No jarring register shifts |
| Formality | 5/10 — no academic jargon, no casual slang | Spot-check 5 random sentences |
| Emotionality | 8/10 — at least 1 emotionally resonant line per scene | Editor marks "flat" scenes |
| Active voice | >80% of sentences use active voice | Passive voice flagged |
| Sentence variety | No 3+ consecutive sentences of same structure | Pattern flagged |
| Sensory detail | Every character scene has 2+ sensory references | Checklist per scene |
| Audio tag density | Max 3-5 tags per 5,000-character segment | Over-tagging flagged |
| Readability | Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level 8-12 (accessible but not dumbed down) | Automated check |

**Output:** Line-edited script with tracked changes. Notes on flat passages, register breaks, and missing sensory detail.

---

### Pass 3 — Copy Edit

**Focus:** Grammar, spelling, factual accuracy, prohibited word enforcement, character count.

**The editor examines:**
- Grammar: subject-verb agreement, pronoun clarity, consistent tense (past tense for historical narration, present for character immersion moments)
- Spelling: all historical names verified (Byzantion not Byzantium in Greek context; Constantinople; Süleymaniye vs Suleymaniye — use anglicized form without diacritics for ElevenLabs compatibility)
- Prohibited words: full scan against NICHE_STYLE_GUIDE Section 4 banned list
- Factual accuracy: dates, population figures, architectural details cross-referenced against research sources
- Anachronisms: no concepts, objects, or language from wrong era
- Content filter check: flag any language that may trigger AI video generation content filters ("siege", "invasion", "conquest", "army") — suggest alternatives
- Character count: verify total narration characters (excluding audio tags) falls within 20,000-22,000 range
- ElevenLabs segment splits: mark natural split points at ~5,000 characters for v3 processing

**Acceptance criteria for Pass 3:**

| Criterion | Standard | Pass/Fail Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Grammar | Zero errors | All flagged and corrected |
| Spelling | All proper nouns verified | Cross-reference list provided |
| Prohibited words | Zero instances of banned words/phrases | Automated scan + manual review |
| Factual accuracy | All dates and figures verified against 2+ sources | Fact-check log maintained |
| Anachronisms | Zero | Each era reviewed for period accuracy |
| Content filter risk | All high-risk phrases flagged with alternatives | Risk register maintained |
| Character count | 20,000-22,000 (narration only, excluding tags) | Counted with tags stripped |
| Segment splits | 4-5 segments of ~5,000 chars each, split at natural pause points | Segment boundaries marked |

**Output:** Clean copy with fact-check log, prohibited word scan results, character count breakdown by scene, and segment split markers.

---

### Pass 4 — Integration Edit

**Focus:** Alignment between narration script, visual prompts, audio direction, and retention devices.

**The editor examines:**
- Does each scene's narration match its visual prompt direction? (No narrating "the fisherman looks up at the dome" if the visual prompt shows a harbor wide shot)
- Are J-cut audio bridges properly indicated in both the narration script and the visual direction?
- Are open loop plants and resolutions clearly marked for the narrator's delivery emphasis?
- Do ElevenLabs audio tags align with the visual mood of each scene?
- Is the pacing of narration compatible with the planned visual tempo? (Slow narration over fast-cut visuals = mismatch)
- Are pattern interrupt moments (every 30-60s) achievable with the planned visual content?
- Does the TELEPROMPTER version strip cleanly — all production notes removed, only narration text + audio tags remain?
- Is the cost estimate compatible with the visual direction? (Premium model assignments for first 3 minutes, economy after)

**Acceptance criteria for Pass 4:**

| Criterion | Standard | Pass/Fail Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Narration-visual sync | Every scene's narration describes or complements its visual direction | Scene-by-scene cross-check |
| J-cut marking | All 3 J-cuts marked in both narration and visual docs | Cross-reference |
| Open loop marking | All 6 loops marked with [PLANT] and [RESOLVE] tags in script | Tag audit |
| Audio tag-visual alignment | No `[excited]` over contemplative visuals; no `[calm]` over action | Mood cross-check |
| Pacing compatibility | Read-aloud timing matches scene duration targets (±15s) | Timed read-through |
| Pattern interrupt feasibility | Visual direction supports a shift every 30-60s | Shot count per scene |
| TELEPROMPTER clean strip | Production notes removed; only narration + tags remain | Strip test |
| Model tier compliance | Scenes 01-05 use PREMIUM tier; Scenes 06+ use ECONOMY (with noted exceptions) | Visual direction audit |

**Output:** Integration-verified final script package: SCRIPT.md (full production script), TELEPROMPTER.md (narration-only), VISUAL_DIRECTION.md (visual prompts per scene), and integration sign-off.

## 2. Acceptance Criteria Summary

### Per-Pass Gate

Each pass must be fully accepted before the next begins. A batch moves forward only when:

| Gate | Condition |
|---|---|
| Pass 1 → Pass 2 | All developmental feedback addressed; structure locked; no further scene reordering |
| Pass 2 → Pass 3 | All line edits accepted or deliberately rejected with rationale; tone verified |
| Pass 3 → Pass 4 | Zero grammar errors; zero prohibited words; character count in range; facts verified |
| Pass 4 → Production | Full integration verified; TELEPROMPTER strips clean; cost-compatible |

### Final Acceptance (Full Script)

The complete 16-scene script is accepted for production when ALL of the following are true:

- [ ] All 4 passes completed for all 3 batches
- [ ] Total narration character count: 20,000-22,000 (excluding audio tags)
- [ ] All 6 open loops planted and resolved
- [ ] All 3 characters fully developed with sensory immersion
- [ ] Zero prohibited words
- [ ] Key insight delivered at Scene 15 with full emotional weight
- [ ] Bosphorus motif present in all 3 eras
- [ ] 1453 framed as transformation, not destruction
- [ ] No Eurocentric framing detected
- [ ] TELEPROMPTER.md ready for ElevenLabs (segments marked, tags placed)
- [ ] Visual direction compatible with budget (Premium Start model)

## 4. Language Register

### Primary Register

**Language:** English
**Register:** BBC documentary narration with intimate warmth
**Formality:** 5/10 (per NICHE_STYLE_GUIDE Section 3)

### Register Description

The narration reads as if spoken by a veteran documentary narrator who has personally walked the streets of Istanbul across centuries. Not a professor lecturing — a storyteller who was there. The language is:

- **Literary but not academic:** Elegant sentence construction without jargon or footnote-language. "The walls that had turned back armies for a thousand years" — not "The Theodosian fortification system, constructed circa 413 CE, proved resistant to numerous siege attempts."
- **Warm but not casual:** The narrator cares about these people. Emotional investment is palpable but controlled. Never sentimental. Never flip.
- **Precise but not dry:** Specific details (dates, names, materials) are embedded in narrative flow, never listed. "By the time Suleiman's architects laid the first stone of the Süleymaniye in 1550..." — the date serves the story, not the other way around.
- **Active, not passive:** "Constantine chose this harbor" — not "This harbor was chosen by Constantine."
- **Varied in rhythm:** Short declarative sentences for impact. Longer, flowing sentences for immersion. Fragments for drama. Never three sentences of the same length in a row.

### Register Examples (in the voice of this video)

**Character introduction (slow, intimate):**
> His name is lost to history. But every morning, before the city woke, he was already at the water's edge — mending nets that his father had first cast into these waters when the harbor had no name.

**Historical context (measured, authoritative):**
> By the sixth century, Constantinople held half a million souls. London, at the same moment, held fifteen thousand. The difference was the strait.

**Dramatic moment (controlled urgency):**
> On the morning of May 29th, 1453, the city that had been Constantinople for eleven centuries woke to a new sound. Not the church bells that had marked its hours for a thousand years — but something else entirely.

**Climax (deliberate, weighted):**
> Three civilizations. Three names. Three skylines built on the same seven hills, fed by the same current, defended by the same walls — or the ruins of them. [pause] No other city on Earth can say this.

### Pronunciation Notes for ElevenLabs

The following terms require attention for correct v3 pronunciation:

| Term | Pronunciation Guide | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Byzantion | "Bih-ZAN-tee-on" | Greek name; distinct from "Byzantium" |
| Bosphorus | "BOS-for-us" | Standard English pronunciation |
| Süleymaniye | "Soo-lay-MAH-nee-yeh" | Use anglicized spelling "Suleymaniye" in script |
| Hagia Sophia | "HAH-gee-ah So-FEE-ah" | May need phonetic respelling for v3 |
| Mese | "MEH-seh" | Byzantine main avenue |
| Mehmed | "Meh-MET" | Sultan Mehmed II |

If v3 mispronounces these terms, use alias rules in an ElevenLabs pronunciation dictionary (.pls file) with phonetic respellings.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Batch 3 | 11-16 | ~7:00 | 8,000-9,000 | 2 segments |
| **Total** | 01-16 | **~17:00** | **20,000-22,000** | **4-5 segments** |

### Character Count Verification Process

1. **After Pass 2 (Line Edit):** Initial character count (narration text only, all `[tags]` stripped)
2. **After Pass 3 (Copy Edit):** Verified character count with segment split markers
3. **After Pass 4 (Integration Edit):** Final count in TELEPROMPTER.md — this is the billing-authoritative number
4. **Overage handling:** If count exceeds 22,000, identify lowest-value sentences for cuts during a targeted Pass 2 revision. Do not cut open loop plants/resolutions, character introductions, or the climax.
5. **Underage handling:** If count falls below 20,000, identify scenes that could benefit from additional sensory detail or historical enrichment. Priority: character scenes (03, 07, 12) and climax (15).
