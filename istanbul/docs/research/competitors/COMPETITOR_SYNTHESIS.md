## Hook Pattern Summary

### Gold Standard Opening (adapt for Istanbul)

Fall of Civilizations, "Byzantium — Last of the Romans":
> *"On the outskirts of modern Istanbul, a line of ancient walls lies crumbling into the earth..."*

**Why it works:** Spatial (outskirts of Istanbul), temporal (modern), visual (walls), decay (crumbling = tension), directional (implies we're going back in time to when they weren't crumbling). Four techniques in one sentence.

### Recommended Hybrid Formula for Istanbul

**Structural pattern:** [Spatial grounding — present day] + [Scale line] + [Zeigarnik question] + [Immediate drop into Era 1]

Example draft:
> *"This strait is never quiet. Even at dawn, before the city wakes, the water moves — carrying ships that have sailed through here for twenty-seven centuries. Today it carries oil tankers and cargo ships. But there was a morning, two thousand seven hundred years ago, when the first boat came through and a man decided: here. Right here. This is where we build.*
>
> *His name is lost to history. But the city he started is still here — and it has been the capital of three civilizations. This is the story of how the water decided everything."*

## Risk Flags from Competitor Analysis

1. **Anachronism risk** — AI generation will default to generic "historic" imagery. Each era's prompt must specify period-accurate details explicitly. Assign a historical accuracy review step.
2. **Format length risk** — At 15-17 minutes, the three-arc structure must deliver a pattern interrupt or open loop every 60-90 seconds or retention will drop below 40% by minute 6. Map retention devices to timestamps during scripting.
3. **Press coverage alignment** — Majestic Studios got press by being the first AI video of 18th-century London. Istanbul has more cultural resonance but the angle must be novel (not "another AI reconstruction of Constantinople" but "the story of the city that changed civilizations three times — told through three ordinary residents").
4. **Scope discipline** — Youcef Hollywood's "all of human history in 20 minutes" shows what happens when scope exceeds depth. Maintain strict focus: one city, three eras, three characters, one strait.
