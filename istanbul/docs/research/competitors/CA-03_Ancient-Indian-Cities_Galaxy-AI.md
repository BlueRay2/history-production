## Strengths to Learn From

1. **Infrastructure as storytelling** — The "what did daily infrastructure look like?" angle generates high curiosity because viewers genuinely don't know. For Istanbul, sections on Grand Bazaar economics, Bosphorus crossing logistics, or water supply to the city (the Valens Aqueduct) could follow this logic to add texture to the narrative.
2. **"ACTUALLY" curiosity frame** — The word "actually" in the title implies that conventional wisdom is wrong, creating immediate curiosity gap. Istanbul equivalent: "Istanbul: The city that's been conquered three times — and thrived every time."
3. **Categorical completeness signals expertise** — Covering 11 aspects builds viewer trust that this creator knows the topic deeply, even in a compressed format.

## Weaknesses to Avoid

1. **Categorical structure without narrative arc** — 11 infrastructure categories in sequence is encyclopedic, not cinematic. Cities Evolution's three-era, three-character structure is fundamentally more engaging.
2. **No emotional anchor** — 59K views for a solid piece of research suggests the format ceiling is modest. Without characters and story, history content taps out at 50K-100K views in this niche.
3. **No chronological evolution** — Showing a single snapshot of a civilization misses the "evolution" dimension. The before/after temporal contrast is Cities Evolution's core engine.

## Relevance to Istanbul Production: Medium
- Confirms: infrastructure-level detail (bazaar economics, water supply, maritime trade logistics) adds texture and authenticity that viewers respond to
- Confirms: pure categorical/informational format without narrative arc has a hard view ceiling
- Key lesson: Use infrastructure details as texture WITHIN the character POV scenes, not as standalone sections
