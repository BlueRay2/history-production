## Strengths to Learn From

1. **Source material approach** — Using actual period paintings/engravings as input gives the AI visual accuracy and consistent aesthetic that pure prompting rarely achieves. For Istanbul: using Byzantine/Ottoman miniatures and historical illustrations as AI input references could yield similarly period-accurate visuals.
2. **Landmark specificity** — Naming recognizable landmarks (London Bridge, Royal Exchange) gives viewers cultural anchors that increase share-ability and comment engagement ("I know that place!")
3. **Press coverage potential** — A well-produced AI history video with a recognizable city can get mainstream press coverage (Londonist, Open Culture, Upworthy). Istanbul has even greater cultural resonance globally.

## Weaknesses to Avoid

1. **No narrative arc** — Pure landmark tour with no character, no story, no open loops. Viewers have no reason to stay past the initial visual curiosity. Cities Evolution's fisherman/merchant/craftsman structure directly solves this.
2. **Static format — single era, single format** — Showing only one time period removes the "evolution" dimension that is Cities Evolution's core differentiator.
3. **"Video game preview" visual quality** — Press specifically used this phrase negatively. Medium-tier AI generation without the premium model pipeline is immediately noticeable to viewers. Investment in Sora 2 for hero shots and the multi-model pipeline is justified by this competitive gap.

## Relevance to Istanbul Production: High
- Most directly comparable to Cities Evolution format (AI-generated historical city)
- Confirms: visual quality floor must exceed "video game preview" to be competitive
- Confirms: cities with iconic landmarks (Hagia Sophia, Grand Bazaar, Bosphorus) have high press coverage potential
- Key lesson: the absence of narrative arc in this format is a competitive OPPORTUNITY — Cities Evolution's character-driven structure is a clear differentiator in this space
