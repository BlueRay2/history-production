## Critical Production Notes

1. **J-cuts at era transitions are non-negotiable.** Scenes 06, 10, and 14 must use audio from the next era before the visual transition. This is the single most important retention technique at the points of highest viewer loss risk.

2. **L5 is a "speed loop"** — planted at 9:30, resolved at 10:00-11:15. It exists specifically to pull the viewer across the Era 2 → Era 3 transition. The viewer chases the payoff across the boundary without noticing the era change.

3. **Scene 12 is an emotional recovery zone.** Do NOT load it with data or intensity. Let the craftsman introduction breathe. The viewer just experienced the 1453 pivot (intensity 9) and needs a valley (intensity 6) before the next climb.

4. **The match cut sequence in Scene 15 is the visual thesis.** Three (or four) identical camera framings of the Bosphorus across eras — Greek fishing boats, Byzantine merchant galleons, Ottoman warships, (implied modern tankers). This is the video's signature visual moment and must receive Flow Quality tier generation despite being in the economy section of the timeline.

5. **No CTA in the first 60 seconds.** The first possible soft CTA window is 9:00 (Scene 09, embedded in narrative). The end-screen CTA (Scene 16) is a value proposition ("future episode on the Bosphorus in the 20th century"), not a request.
