## Vision Statement

A 15-17 minute cinematic documentary that tells the story of Istanbul through the lives of three ordinary people across three empires — a Greek fisherman, a Byzantine silk merchant, and an Ottoman tile-maker — connected by the Bosphorus strait, the body of water that made each empire inevitable.

## Core Thesis

Istanbul is the only city in history that became the capital of three completely different empires. And each time, it was the same 31-kilometer strait that decided its fate.

## Creative Direction

### Narrative Approach
- **Character-driven, not architecture-driven.** Every era is told through one person's daily life. The city transforms around them.
- **Transformation, not destruction.** 1453 is a reinvention, not a fall. Mehmed II preserved Hagia Sophia. The Grand Bazaar survived all three civilizations.
- **The Bosphorus as protagonist.** The strait is the connecting thread — same water, same current, same fish pushed into the Golden Horn for 2,700 years. Visual match cuts across eras.

### Emotional Arc
Intimacy (fisherman's quiet morning) → Grandeur (Constantinople at its peak, 500K vs London's 15K) → Transformation (1453 as the city's third reinvention) → Transcendence (the Bosphorus remembers all of it).

### Selected Hook (Recommended: Hook B — Double Hook)
Modern fisherman at Galata Bridge at dawn → camera reveals the 2,700-year skyline → "This man is doing exactly what men have done on this shore for twenty-seven centuries."
- Matches Fall of Civilizations gold-standard: spatial-temporal grounding
- Thumbnail synergy: fisherman + Istanbul skyline = immediate visual promise fulfilled
- Creates bookend structure (Scene 01 → Scene 16)

### Three Characters
1. **Greek Fisherman (~657 BC)** — sensory, intimate. Nets, salt air, wooden hull, dawn light. The Golden Horn as fishing paradise. Premium AI tier (Sora 2 for close-ups).
2. **Byzantine Silk Merchant (~1000 AD)** — cosmopolitan, scale. The Mese boulevard, gold solidus, Hagia Sophia's glow. Economy AI tier (medium/wide shots).
3. **Ottoman Tile-Maker (~1550 AD)** — transformation made tangible. Iznik ceramics, Suleymaniye construction, the Grand Bazaar endures. Economy AI tier (hands, silhouettes).

### Key Research Insights (from SOURCE_MATERIALS.md)
- "City of the Blind" — Herodotus legend about colonists ignoring the Golden Horn. Strong narrative hook.
- Gold solidus — 98% purity for 700 years. World's reserve currency. Pattern interrupt data point.
- 70 ships dragged overland on greased planks (1453). Cinematic visual.
- Grand Bazaar — 565 years continuous operation, 91 million visitors/year. Continuity personified.
- Orban's cannon — 27 feet long, 1,200 lb stone shot, 7 shots/day. Visual spectacle.

### Retention Strategy
- 6 open loops across 16 scenes, never more than 2 active simultaneously
- Pattern interrupts every 30-60 seconds (scale shifts, data overlays, temporal flash-forwards)
- J-cuts between era transitions (fisherman's harbor audio → merchant's voice)
- Emotional contagion via character stories + ElevenLabs warmth
- Target average retention: 48-55%

### Visual Style
Warm taupe, sandy brown, warm umber, cream white. Shot on 35mm film, shallow DOF, visible grain. Golden undertones, aged parchment feel. Premium Start (Sora 2 + Flow Quality first 3 min), Flow Fast (free) after.

### Audio Direction
- Patrick International (ElevenLabs v3), documentary warmth with character intimacy
- Music: lyre/oud (Greek) → orchestral + choir (Byzantine) → ney/kanun (Ottoman) → all motifs woven (climax)
- Silence beats before era transitions
