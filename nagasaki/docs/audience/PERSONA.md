# Audience Persona — Nagasaki Video

**Channel:** Cities Evolution — AI-reconstructed cinematic documentaries of great Asian port cities, each era viewed through an ordinary resident (fisherman, merchant, craftsman).

## Primary viewer — "The Informed Lurker"

- **Age:** 25–45
- **Gender split:** ~60% M / 40% F
- **Geography:** United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Western Europe, Southeast Asia (English-speaking + Asian diaspora)
- **Education:** 4-year degree or in progress; broad intellectual curiosity; reads long-form (Aeon, Atlantic, NYT Magazine) but does not specialize in East Asian history academically.
- **Work context:** Knowledge-economy roles (software, finance, consulting, design, academia-adjacent), lean-back evening viewing on Smart TV / tablet.
- **Relationship with Asia:** Visited Kyoto or Hong Kong as a tourist, or planning to. Has read *Silence* by Endō Shūsaku OR seen the 2016 Scorsese film OR watched *Shōgun* (2024 FX remake). Japan is on their "places I want to understand better" list.
- **Genre literacy:** Has watched Kurzgesagt, Veritasium, Real Engineering, and 3–5 AI-reconstructed history channels. Can immediately tell "AI slop" from curated AI.

## Psychographic drivers

- **Values:** intellectual curiosity, cross-cultural empathy, visual aesthetic (hates "cheap CGI" look), anti-orientalism, anti-Eurocentrism.
- **Fears:** being fed factually wrong history dressed in pretty AI-footage; orientalist stereotypes; emotional manipulation without payoff.
- **Aspirations:** "I want to *see* a Dutch trader stepping off a junk in 1740s Nagasaki, understand what he smelled and feared, and come away knowing something a Wikipedia summary wouldn't tell me."
- **Viewing context:** evening (post-dinner, 20:00–23:00 local), alone or with partner, phone down, Smart TV or laptop.

## Pain points with competing Nagasaki content on YouTube

- "Top 10 things you didn't know about Nagasaki" — shallow, listicle format, no human voice.
- Straight WWII documentaries — cover the atomic bombing only, skip the 300 years before.
- Christianity-in-Japan content — academic/Catholic-outlet audience, low production value.
- Travel vlogs — present-day shots of the Peace Park, no historical depth.

**The gap:** Nobody is telling the story *all at once* — Dejima's two-century isolation + the kakure kirishitan's 250-year silence + the atomic bombing of the most Christian square kilometer in Asia — as a single braided narrative with character anchors and cinematic AI reconstruction. That's the positioning.

## What this persona will NOT tolerate

- Hey-guys-welcome-back greetings → hard abandon.
- Mispronunciation of Japanese place names (Nagasaki, Urakami, Dejima, Kakure, Orasho, Hibakusha).
- Uncredited re-use of stock footage dated post-1950.
- "Christianity good, Shogunate bad" moral flattening — they want complexity.
- Nagai's *hansai-setsu* ("holocaust theory") presented uncritically — they know it's controversial.
- Mushroom cloud as cheap emotional hook.

## What this persona will love

- Sugimoto Yuri / Isabelina's first words in 1865 — "our breast is the same as yours" — delivered in a voice that treats it as the sacred moment it was.
- Hendrik Doeff refusing to lower the Dutch flag on Dejima 1810–1815 (the Napoleonic-era solo-sovereignty detail).
- The rosary in Midori Nagai's hand-bones — a detail no other 8-minute Nagasaki video surfaces.
- Kokura weather diversion — the bomb missed its target by 3 km and landed on the Christian quarter by accident of cumulus cover.
- Fr. George Zabelka's 1975 reversal — the man who blessed Bockscar turning peace activist.
