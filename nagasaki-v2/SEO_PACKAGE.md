## Titles (5 variants)

All titles: ≤ 60 chars, ≥ 1 primary keyword, anti-clickbait compliant, persona rationale provided.

### Title 1 — Transformation-promise angle (RECOMMENDED, default)
> **"The 250-Year Secret Beneath the Nagasaki Bomb"**
- **Chars:** 48
- **Keywords:** "Nagasaki bomb"
- **Persona rationale:** Matches "unexpected historical continuity" trigger directly. Uses physical metaphor ("beneath") to hook both shock reveals without using the words "hidden Christians" (which don't search as strongly alone). Strong curiosity gap without clickbait.
- **Risk:** "Beneath" could be misread as literal underground. Acceptable — scene 1 resolves.

### Title 2 — Identity-signaling angle (for "Japan-curious" secondary persona)
> **"Japan's Hidden Christians Survived 250 Years — Then 1945"**
- **Chars:** 58
- **Keywords:** "Hidden Christians Japan", "1945" (Nagasaki-bomb-adjacent)
- **Persona rationale:** Direct keyword match for primary search. "Then 1945" lands the second shock without stating it. Respects audience intelligence.
- **Risk:** slightly long title — YouTube may truncate to ~56 chars on mobile.

### Title 3 — In Medias Res angle
> **"At 11:02, Their Hidden Faith Ended in 43 Seconds"**
- **Chars:** 50
- **Keywords:** no primary keyword (weakest SEO, strongest hook)
- **Persona rationale:** For viewers who already know Nagasaki — delivers the 43-second hook as title. Performs best on recommendations (CTR), worst on search.
- **Use case:** if A/B testing shows Titles 1-2 underperform in first 48h, switch to this for algorithmic push.

### Title 4 — UNESCO / travel halo angle (riding 2026 NYT pick)
> **"Nagasaki's UNESCO Secret: 250 Years of Hiding"**
- **Chars:** 46
- **Keywords:** "UNESCO", "Nagasaki", "hiding" (proxy for hidden)
- **Persona rationale:** captures Japan-curious secondary persona searching 2026 travel. Softer emotional hook, safer for wider audience.
- **Risk:** does not reference the atomic bomb — may misrepresent scope. Use only if we expand to a 2-part series.

### Title 5 — Character-anchor angle
> **"The Doctor Who Found His Wife's Rosary in the Ash"**
- **Chars:** 50
- **Keywords:** none primary (pure narrative hook)
- **Persona rationale:** For Need-for-Cognition audience — personal-stakes hook over event-name hook. Lowest search discoverability, highest CTR from thumbnail pairing.
- **Use case:** A/B variant or Shorts spinoff title.
