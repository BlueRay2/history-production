## Target retention curve

**Shape:** Logarithmic decay, base 0.7 — steep initial drop from 100% to ~75% in first 60 seconds (normal attrition), then stabilize with three plateau-and-rise bumps at each Act boundary.

**Target numbers (from Cities Evolution competitor benchmarks):**
- t=0:15: ≥ 92% (hook completion)
- t=1:00: ≥ 75% (past Act I setup)
- t=3:30: ≥ 58% (Act II reveal)
- t=6:00: ≥ 45% (Act III intro)
- t=9:00: ≥ 38% (final payoff)
- **Average view duration target:** 60-65% of total length (well above channel baseline ~45%)

## Shepard-tone application

The video creates an illusion of continuous forward motion despite its 250-year + 80-year time jumps. Technique:
- Each Act opens with a "return to the valley" wide shot (same landscape, different era) — visual Shepard-tone loop.
- Bell motif: Act I's Dejima gate-bell → Act II's cathedral bells → Act III's melted bell found in ruins → coda's modern bell ringing. Audio Shepard — each bell sounds like "the same bell" despite centuries between.
- Chorale motif: Orasho (oral, primitive, a capella in Act I) → full choir at Urakami 1914 → ash-silent in Act III → returns in coda. Audio continuity binds discontinuous time.

## Hook script variants (for Phase 2 selection)

**Variant A — Double Hook (SELECTED)**
> "In 1865, a group of Japanese peasants walked into a newly-built Catholic church in Nagasaki and confessed a secret their families had protected for two hundred and fifty years. Eighty years later — at eleven oh two, on a bright August morning — a second sun opened directly above that same church. The oldest hidden Christian community on earth was erased in forty-three seconds."

**Variant B — In Medias Res**
> "Urakami, nine August nineteen forty-five, eleven oh two in the morning. A doctor at the medical college one kilometre away feels the world go white. He will walk home to his wife. He will find her rosary in the ash — the only part of her that did not burn. Her family had carried that rosary for ten generations, in secret, through an empire that tried for two and a half centuries to erase it."

**Variant C — Curiosity Gap + Hyper-specificity**
> "There is a bell in Nagasaki that has rung on three different continents of time. It was cast in nineteen fourteen, it melted in nineteen forty-five, and it rings again today. The story of how that bell survived — and of the people who built it, hid it, died for it, and rebuilt it — begins four hundred years ago on an island the size of a parking lot."

**Recommended:** **A** for universal-audience pull; **B** as 2nd choice for in-medias-res preference; **C** strongest if we want the "bell as object" motif to be the primary hook.
